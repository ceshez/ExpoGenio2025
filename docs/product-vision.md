# Product Vision - Genio

## What Genio is

Genio is a no-code / low-code website builder that helps entrepreneurs, small businesses, local stores, freelancers, and service providers create professional websites without needing to write code.

The product combines a visual drag-and-drop editor, reusable website sections, publishing, authentication, and future business tools such as product management, inventory, customer requests, and AI-assisted page generation.

## Core problem

Many small businesses need a professional online presence, but they often lack the time, budget, design skills, or technical knowledge required to build and maintain a website.

Genio solves this by giving users a guided visual experience where they can create, edit, publish, and manage their websites from one platform.

## Target users

Primary users:

- Small businesses and local stores.
- Entrepreneurs and self-employed workers.
- Service providers.
- Freelancers.
- Photographers and creatives.
- Small online sellers.

Secondary users:

- Students or beginners who need a simple site.
- Community projects or small organizations.
- People who need a landing page quickly.

## Market direction

Genio should feel especially useful for Costa Rican and Latin American small businesses that need a simple but professional digital presence.

The product should be friendly, accessible, and visually polished, while still being strong enough to evolve into a SaaS platform.

## Core promise

A non-technical user should be able to create and publish a professional website quickly, using visual components and guided customization.

## Product principles

- Simple before complex.
- Visual before technical.
- Friendly for non-technical users.
- Professional enough for real businesses.
- Scalable enough to become a SaaS.
- Fast to start, flexible to grow.
- Useful output, not just a pretty editor.
- Every feature should help users publish, sell, manage, or improve their online presence.

## Main product areas

### Dashboard

The dashboard is the control center where users manage their sites, recent designs, favorites, deleted items, templates, account settings, and future business tools.

### Visual editor

The editor is powered by Puck. Users can build pages by adding, editing, arranging, and publishing reusable sections.

### Published websites

Published sites render saved page content publicly. They should be fast, responsive, and visually consistent.

### Templates

Templates should help users start faster. They should be organized by use case, such as store, service business, portfolio, restaurant, personal brand, or landing page.

### Store and inventory tools

Future store features should allow users to manage products, categories, stock, and customer requests. The first versions should remain simple and focused on small businesses.

### AI assistance

AI should help users create pages, improve text, suggest sections, generate layouts, and speed up the website creation process. AI should guide the user, not replace their control.

## Future SaaS direction

Genio can evolve into a SaaS platform with:

- User plans and limits.
- Published site limits.
- AI credit usage.
- Template marketplace.
- Store/product management.
- Custom domains.
- Analytics.
- Team collaboration.
- Customer request management.

## What Genio should not become

Genio should not become overly technical for the user.

Avoid:

- Exposing unnecessary developer terms in the UI.
- Making users configure complex settings before publishing.
- Adding features that do not help users create, manage, publish, or grow their website.
- Building a complex e-commerce system before the basic site creation and publishing flow is solid.

## Product quality bar

A Genio feature is good when it is:

- Easy to understand.
- Useful for a real small business.
- Consistent with the visual identity.
- Safe for user data.
- Scalable for future SaaS features.
- Simple enough to maintain.
